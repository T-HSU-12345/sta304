Non-technical Executive Summary

Introduction

This survey seeks to determine the effects of Daylight Saving Time policy on the population, and their respective opinions on the subject. To clarify, Day Light Saving time is where on two days of the year we shift the clock forward one hour, and later backwards one hour. The original purpose of this is to make more use out of daytime during the summer months, as it moves one hour of daylight in the morning to the evening.

In this report, the data is gathered with an online form targeting the populations of Saskatchewan and Ontario. These two provinces are chosen because one uses DST (Ontario), and the other doesn’t (Saskatchewan). However, Saskatchewan is always on DST. Meaning that the time zone for Saskatchewan should be Mountain Time Zone (GST -7:00), but it is using Central Standard Time (GST -6:00).
